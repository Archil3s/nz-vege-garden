## Region notes

### Northland

- Warmest region
- Low frost risk in many coastal areas
- Long growing season
- High humidity can increase fungal disease pressure

### Auckland

- Mild winters
- Humid summers
- Good year-round growing potential
- Slugs, snails, and fungal issues can be common

### Waikato / Bay of Plenty

- Productive vegetable region
- Mild to moderate frost depending on location
- Humid conditions in many areas

### Gisborne / Hawke's Bay

- Warm and dry in many areas
- Good summer crop potential
- Irrigation and water management important

### Taranaki / Manawatū

- Moist, temperate conditions
- Wind exposure varies by site
- Slug/snail and fungal pressure can be relevant

### Wellington / Wairarapa

- Wellington: wind exposure is a major factor
- Wairarapa: hotter summers and colder winters
- Frost risk varies significantly

### Nelson / Marlborough

- Good sunshine hours
- Dry summers in many places
- Watering and mulching important

### Canterbury

- Cool winters
- Frost risk is important
- Hot, dry summers possible
- Wind protection helpful for seedlings

### West Coast

- High rainfall
- Mild temperatures
- Drainage and fungal management important

### Otago

- Strong seasonal variation
- Frost risk important
- Central Otago has hot summers and cold winters

### Southland

- Cool climate
- Shorter warm season
- Frost-sensitive crops need protection and timing care
